Baltimore Open Data Resource Guide

Open Baltimore

https://data.baltimorecity.gov Open Baltimore is the City of Baltimore's Open Data Catalog. You can explore city-wide data on information such as crime, city employee salaries, minority-ownred businesses, parking citations, vacant lots, and public art installations in the city.

Baltimore Neighborhood Indicators Alliance (BNIA)

http://bniajfl.org BNIA aggregates community-level data on demographics housing and community development, crime and safety, and other community Vital Signs. You can download the data in easy to use, visual data formats that are segmented by Baltimore neighborhoods.

Baltimore DataMind

http://www.baltimoredatamind.org/map Baltimore DataMind has a platform that you can use to navigate through the city and quickly see neighborhood-level data on demographics, health, housing, and crime and safety.

Baltimore City Health Department

http://www.baltimorehealth.org/neighborhoodmap.html The Baltimore City Health Department releases neighborhood health profiles in .pdf format, which compares neighborhood-level data against Baltimore City data on demographic information, social determinants of health by neighborhood, and health outcomes.

The Sunlight Foundation

https://sunlightfoundation.com The Sunlight Foundation is a nonpartisan nonprofit organization with the goal of increasing transparency and accountability in the US Congress, the executive branch, and in state and local governments. You can explore the tools and projects of the Sunlight Foundation here and the released APIs here.

Census Bureau

http://factfinder2.census.gov/faces/nav/jsf/pages/index.xhtml Search through Census decennial data of general population and housing characteristics (population, age, sex, race, households, and housing). American Community Survey data (ACS) data is also available for statistical economic, social, and housing characteristics. Data available for download ranged from state level to Census tract to block level data. The FactFinder tool enables users to create maps at general population levels, modify tables to statistics of interest, and download the data.

Twitter

https://dev.twitter.com You can use the Twitter API to grab location-based tweets by topic, hashtag, and other kinds of filters.
